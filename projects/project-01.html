The original guide track was delivered completely silent — just the picture cut and rough dialogue. The challenge was to build an oppressive interior world from scratch: the hum of fluorescent lights, distant footsteps distorted through thin walls, and a growing sub-frequency pressure that signals the protagonist's psychological unraveling.

Approach & Intent

The brief called for "claustrophobia without jump scares" — so all sounds were treated to feel like they came from inside the protagonist's head. Convolution reverbs sampled in real hospital corridors, granular-stretched breathing textures, and a slowly de-tuning room tone were the core palette. Everything was mixed in 5.1 surround then folded to stereo for delivery.
