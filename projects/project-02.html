The studio shipped the game with generic asset-pack sound effects — flat, looping engine drones and single-hit collision sounds. The brief was to replace the entire audio system with something reactive: sound that changes based on RPM, drift angle, surface type, and the player's speed relative to their personal best lap.

Before shows the original build with placeholder SFX. After demonstrates the FMOD-integrated reactive system where engine pitch, reverb send, and music layer density all modulate dynamically with gameplay state.

Approach & Intent

The engine sound was built from scratch in Serum — four oscillators tuned to harmonics of a real four-cylinder engine, pitch-mapped to an RPM parameter in FMOD. Surface materials trigger different reverb convolution IRs: asphalt, tunnel, and open desert. The music system uses five stems that cross-fade based on the player's position relative to their ghost car, creating a natural tension arc without a single line of narrative code.
